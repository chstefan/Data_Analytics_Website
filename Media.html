TED Talks

Jer Thorp: "Make data more human"

Jer Thorp's TED Talk, "Make data more human," brings data to life by transforming numbers into engaging stories. He showcases projects that reveal human patterns in news cycles and social sharing, turning complex datasets into visual narratives that resonate on a personal level. Thorp's work beautifully illustrates how data can connect deeply with our daily experiences and emotions.

Steve Brown: "How data is driving the future of fashion"

In his TED Talk Steve Brown discusses the transformative impact of data on the fashion industry. He highlights how designers are harnessing data from diverse sources like Instagram, runway shows, and even changing rooms to stay ahead of trends and streamline their creative processes. Contrary to fears that data might stifle creativity, Brown argues that it actually frees designers to focus more on innovation and design by making production cycles more efficient.

Vikram Sharma: "How quantum physics can make encryption stronger"

Vikram Sharma explores the critical role of quantum physics in fortifying encryption against the looming threats posed by quantum computing. As computational power increases, our current encryption methods become vulnerable, but Sharma discusses how we can use quantum technology itself to create security systems that are resilient against these advanced attacks. He emphasizes the urgency of developing these quantum-resistant encryption techniques to safeguard our data and democratic processes in the near future.
